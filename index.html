About

I am a student in mathematical physics at the University of Vienna. My research interest lies in mathematical general relativity, with emphasis on spacetime inextendibility, gravitational collapse, black holes and naked singularities. I am particularly interested in low-regularity Lorentzian geometry, and geometric and analytical methods in general relativity. More broadly, the problems that motivate me most are those in which one seeks a result that is simultaneously mathematically solid and physically interpretable.

Research

Inextendibility of Spacetimes

I work on questions of low-regularity inextendibility in general relativity, especially for physically relevant collapse spacetimes. A central theme is the interaction between causal structure, singular behavior, and obstructions to continuous or low-regularity extensions. Currently I am working on proving the C0-inextendibility of Vaidya spacetimes as part of my masters thesis under the supervision of Clemens Sämann.

Gravitational Collapse and Naked Singularities

I am interested in geometric and analytic questions arising from gravitational collapse, including black hole formation, visibility of singularities, and conditions under which collapse leads to globally or locally naked singular behavior.

Lorentzian Length Spaces

I am interested in synthetic and low-regularity approaches to Lorentzian geometry, especially the extent to which classical causality and singularity theory can be formulated beyond the smooth setting.

Geometric and Analytical Methods in Relativity

More broadly, I am drawn to problems in which differential geometry, causal theory, and analysis jointly clarify physically interpretable phenomena in spacetime geometry.
